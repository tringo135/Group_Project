  The healthcare management software project presents several potential risks that need to be identified and managed adequately as a means to ensure a successful project. One of the significant risks to consider is the lack of proficiency in handing out health advice. The main function of this system is to collect health data from users and then it would provide reliable health advice based on the collected data. Without sufficient knowledge of medical advice, the software could offer misleading advice which might cause harm to the customers. Another risk is cybersecurity threats, which has potential affect this project negatively. As health data are all sensitive and private information, data breaches would be a huge problem since the data can be used with malicious intent, which results in severe consequences. Thus, it’s crucial for us to make sure that the software has ways to prevent the threats from any unauthorized access. Moreover, regulatory compliance might be an issue for this project. Many people know that healthcare is a heavily regulated industry. Any failure to act in accordance with legal regulations would end in legal consequences. That’s why the project team must oversee the software carefully and check whether it conformed with all regulatory standards and undergo regular audits. This process might change some requirements in the projects, which cause another risk affecting the project’s timeline. In other words, changes in requirements result in delaying the time to finish the software. We think we can mitigate that risk with proper project planning and regular meetings. However, we cannot deny the unexpected factors that would appear during the project.
